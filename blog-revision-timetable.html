Study Planning

The Ultimate 30-Day Revision Timetable Template

A structured 30-day revision plan helps students cover important topics steadily and avoid last-minute panic.

A revision timetable works best when it is realistic, balanced, and easy to follow. Instead of trying to study everything at once, students can use a 30-day plan that slowly builds confidence and covers the syllabus in a manageable way.

Why a 30-day revision plan helps

When a student studies without a clear plan, revision often becomes random and stressful. A timetable creates structure, ensures all subjects are covered, and gives space for practice, rest, and review.

How to build the timetable

  • • Divide the month into daily study blocks for each subject.
  • • Give more time to weak topics but keep easier revision short and consistent.
  • • Include one day each week for review and mistake correction.

A simple weekly structure

A balanced daily timetable can include homework, focused study, practice questions, and short breaks. The goal is not to study for many hours but to study with focus and consistency.

Tips for making it work

Keep the schedule visible, review it every evening, and adjust it if a plan is too ambitious. Even a simple timetable is useful when it is followed regularly and updated as needed.

Why this helps

  • • Creates structure for revision
  • • Reduces stress before exams
  • • Helps students track progress clearly
